Projects

In this portfolio I will concentrate on my free-time software projects.

  Project: Share Contact - Share Contact is a Windows Phone 7/8 application that provides an easy way to share your contact information with other mobile devices. It generates a QR code from your info and shares your contact information with other mobile devices by using global vcard standard. User interaction is simple. The user inserts their own information at the first startup and after that the user is able to share their info simply by asking the other person to scan their contact QR code. Tech: C#, C++, Windows Phone 7/8
  Project: Water Usage Monitoring - Our water usage monitorin solution analysed water consumption by water pipes vibrations and reported the results to AWS cloud service. The product was implemented from a scratch to Nordic Independent Living Challenge. The main rationale behind the idea was to improve elders safety by following their daily water consumption. Tech: hardware designing, Arduino C, C#, .NET, AWS
  Project: Sparkling Wine Robot - We got an idea of building a Sparkling Wine Robot for the first of May. So, first we got our sponsor HiQ with us and they helped us to get this project forward. I was responsible of project overall management so I would describe myself as a project coordinator. The project included really intense two weeks. We got the mechanical robot with motors and sensors from Tampere but it was missing a powersupply and drivers. So, we designed to implement those on our own. Project schedule was tight because we had to wait a few weeks our first hardware batch from China. Middle of the project we noticed schema errors in our circuit boards so we had to fix quite many circuitboards manually. We also got a side project by simulating the robot movements on PC. For this, we downloaded the robot's 3D model and coded a simple OpenGL simulation of the robot. We were able to set the robot into different positions and simulate the robot movements. Tech: Arduino C, Unix, OpenGL, C++, Math: Inverse Kinematics, Control Theory, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Design, 3D printing More information about the project: http://sooda.dy.fi/2015/6/7/robokasi-skumppaa-kaatamassa-wappuna/ http://tkln.fi/projects/_control_system_for_puma_560/Free-time project, 2015 Event: https://twitter.com/hiqfinland/status/594051189270061057 https://www.facebook.com/HiQFinland/posts/903057573084948 Making-of video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U5I2BmSMImA Team: Aapo Vienamo, Aapo Oksman, Eero Santamala, Joel Huttunen, Juho Kortekangas, Konsta Hölttä, Miika Lehtimäki, Petri Niemelä, Sami-Petteri Pukkila and Veikka Kähkönen

  Project: Canny-Sobel Edge Detection - Canny-Sobel Edge Detection is commonly used in image processing. The algorithm is detecting edges by calculating vertical and horisontal color gradients from a picture. After the gradients, the algorithm uses a soft and hard neighbour mapping to narrow down the edges. I implemented the algorithm by using C++11 and optimized it to work well with large pictures.
  Project: Wine Classification Challenge - I participated to a machine learning challenge where the task was to rate quality of thousands of wines based on their chemical properties. We got 1000 expert analysis from different wines and their chemical properties as a training set. We ended up using a multivariable support vector machine (SVM) method, neural networks, and K-nearest neighboards algorithms to solve this problem. We applied different kernel functions and eventually ended up mainly using SVM as our final solution. Tech: Matlab
  Project: Tetris by Pixels - I participated to a fast game compo where limitations were that no media was allowed. So, I decided to implement a tetris game where all graphics are generated from different fractals. Tech: Coolbasic
  Project: Line Following Car, AVR - Line Following Car was a school project where we implemented a small low level AVR program to keep the car on a white line. The car had eight infra-red sensors and the path was marked on a white tape. We implemented a Kalman filter and a PID-controller to keep the vehicle on the track and also monitored the vehicle's battery voltage to adjust the thrust to the car's energy. Team: Joel Huttunen, Mikko Rekola, and Tarmo Räntilä Tech: AVR, C, Control Theory
