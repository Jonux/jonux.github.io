  Project: Wine Classification Challenge
Autumn 2015
Tech: Matlab

I participated to a machine learning challenge where the task was to rate quality of thousands of wines based on their chemical properties. We got 1000 expert analysis from different wines and their chemical properties as a training set.
We ended up using a multivariable support vector machine (SVM) method, neural networks, and K-nearest neighboards algorithms to solve this problem. We applied different kernel functions and eventually ended up using SVM in our final solution.

  Project: Canny-Sobel Edge Detection
Summer 2015
Tech: C++11

Canny-Sobel Edge Detection is commonly used in image processing. The algorithm is detecting edges by calculating vertical and horisontal color gradients from a picture. After the gradients, the algorithm uses a soft and hard neighbour mapping to narrow down the edges.

  Project: Sparkling Wine Robot
Spring 2015
Tech: Arduino C, Unix, OpenGL, C++, Math: Inverse Kinematics, Control Theory, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Design, 3D printing
Team: Aapo Vienamo, Aapo Oksman, Eero Santamala, Joel Huttunen, Juho Kortekangas, Konsta Hölttä, Miika Lehtimäki, Petri Niemelä, Sami-Petteri Pukkila and Veikka Kähkönen

We got an idea of building a Sparkling Wine Robot for the first of May. So, first we got our sponsor HiQ with us and they helped us to get this project forward. I was responsible of project overall management so I would describe myself as a project coordinator.

The project included really intense two weeks. We got the mechanical robot with motors and sensors from Tampere but it was missing multiple parts, like a powersupply, controlling unit, and mechanical gripper. So, we had to implement those on our own.

Project schedule was tight because we had to wait a couple of weeks for our first hardware batch from China. Middle of the project, we noticed schema errors in our circuit boards so we had to fix quite many circuitboards manually.

We also got a side project by simulating the robot movements on PC. For this, we downloaded the robot's 3D model and implemented a simple OpenGL simulation of the robot's movements. Eventually, the project was a success! We were able to move the robot by driving it by set points. So, we were ready to start serving sparkling wine just before the event started!

  Project: Line Following Car, AVR
Spring 2015
Tech: AVR, C, Control Theory
Team: Joel Huttunen, Mikko Rekola, and Tarmo Räntilä

Line Following Car was a school project where we implemented a small low level AVR program to keep the car on a white line. The car had eight infra-red sensors and the path was marked on a white tape. We implemented a Kalman filter and a PID-controller to keep the vehicle on the track. We also monitored the vehicle's battery voltage and took it into account in control values evaluation.

  Project: Share Contact
Summer 2013
Tech: C#, C++, Windows Phone 7/8

Share Contact is a Windows Phone 7/8 application that provides an easy way to share your contact information with other mobile devices. It generates a QR code from your info and shares your contact information with other mobile devices by using global vcard standard.
User interaction is simple. The user inserts their own information at the first startup and after that the user is able to share their info simply by asking the other person to scan their contact QR code.
